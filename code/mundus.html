Description

Mundus is a (WIP) project to develop a real-time interactive planet simulation. It is coded entirely from scratch (no middleware engine) in C++, using SDL2 and OpenGL.

fig. 1
Mundus Planet Simulator

Plate Tectonics

Plate tectonics are simulated on the surface of the planet, modeling plate collision, subduction zones, volcanoes, and erosion.

fig. 1
Plate Tectonics

Height data is stored in a geodesic grid, and is updated every frame by the plate tectonics simulation. As plates crash into each other, one plate is pushed (subducted) under the other and crust is added to the border, building up mountain ranges over time.

When plates pull away from each other, a very thin crust is created in the gap, resulting in new oceans, trenches and volcanoes.

fig. 1
Plate Tectonics

Erosion is processed per frame after the tectonics simulation, rounding out the sharp geological features created by plate tectonics.

Surface Temperature

Surface temperature is simulated based on axial-tilt, and orbital period. Seasonal variances are apparent, and ice caps form and recede as the planet orbits the sun.

Biology

Life is simulated per cell as a cellular automaton, and is influenced by surface temperature, water level and annual sunshine.
